ITEM 1. BUSINESS

Solstice Robotics, Inc. ("Solstice," "we," "us," or "the Company") designs
and manufactures autonomous mobile robots for warehouse logistics. We
operate through two reportable segments: Hardware Systems, which sells
robot units and docking infrastructure, and Software & Services, which
provides the fleet-management platform and ongoing maintenance contracts
our customers rely on after purchase.

ITEM 1A. RISK FACTORS

Our revenue is concentrated among a small number of large customers. In
fiscal year 2025, our three largest customers accounted for approximately
41% of total revenue, and the loss of any one of them could materially
affect our results of operations.

We depend on a limited number of suppliers for lidar sensors and
specialized motor controllers. A sustained disruption at any single
supplier could delay shipments and harm our relationships with customers
who have committed deployment schedules.

Our Software & Services segment has grown faster than Hardware Systems
and now carries higher gross margins, but it also depends on customer
renewal rates. A decline in net dollar retention would have a
disproportionate effect on operating margin given the segment's growing
share of total revenue.

ITEM 7. MANAGEMENT'S DISCUSSION AND ANALYSIS

Total revenue for fiscal year 2025 was $412.6 million, an increase of 18%
compared to $349.7 million in fiscal year 2024. Hardware Systems revenue
was $268.3 million, up 9% year over year, while Software & Services
revenue was $144.3 million, up 41% year over year.

Gross margin for the year was 47.2%, compared to 44.1% in the prior year.
The improvement was driven primarily by the increasing mix of
Software & Services revenue, which carries a gross margin of
approximately 78%, versus Hardware Systems gross margin of approximately
31%.

Operating expenses, excluding cost of revenue, were $156.8 million,
representing 38.0% of total revenue, compared to 41.2% in the prior year.
Research and development expense was $71.4 million, or 17.3% of revenue.

Net income for fiscal year 2025 was $24.9 million, compared to a net loss
of $6.2 million in fiscal year 2024. The improvement reflects both the
revenue growth described above and continued operating leverage as fixed
costs were spread over a larger revenue base.

As of the end of fiscal year 2025, we had cash and cash equivalents of
$187.3 million and no outstanding debt. We believe our existing cash
balance, together with cash generated from operations, will be sufficient
to fund operations for at least the next 24 months.

Annual recurring revenue from Software & Services subscriptions was
$118.7 million at year end, representing a net dollar retention rate of
112% for the trailing twelve months.

ITEM 7A. QUANTITATIVE AND QUALITATIVE DISCLOSURES ABOUT MARKET RISK

We are exposed to foreign currency risk related to manufacturing
operations in Mexico and Vietnam. Approximately 22% of our cost of
revenue is denominated in currencies other than the U.S. dollar. We do
not currently use derivative instruments to hedge this exposure.
